Course Description

Metadata plays a critical role in the creation, distribution, management and use of physical and electronic resources/materials. This course will combine theoretical examination of the design of metadata schema with their practical application. Hands-on experience in the creation of descriptive, administrative and structural metadata, performing metadata crosswalking, and developing a metadata will help students gain a thorough understanding of current metadata standards, how different metadata schemas can or do relate to one another, and an introduction to the less-practical aspects - the social and poltical aspects - of metadata creation and schema design.


Required Text

Course Goals

The purposes or goals of the course are to

  • introduce concepts and challenges specific to metadata creation and schema development,
  • expose student to the varieties of metadata schemas for networked resources,
  • promote reasoned thinking and problem solving skills related to information organization, and
  • acquaint the student with current trends in metadata management and control.

Goals for Student Learning

At the conclusion of the course, the student will be able to:

  • understand the complex characteristics of cultural resources and material and the major issues involved in organizing, describing, and providing access to them,
  • articulate ways in which diverse metadata schemas and standards are used to organize different kinds of online resources in different communities and contexts,
  • become familiar with current metadata terminology and encoding schemas, and
  • create metadata for digital resources.

Brief assignment descriptions:

Reading summaries, online discussion, online lab work : Students will be required to write short response papers of readings for 5 individual weeks and post those responses to the course website for peer discussion. Students not posting weekly responses are expected to nevertheless be fully engaged in wider discussion. There will sometimes be lab tasks; all students will be expected to do these and post output to course website.

Metadata encoding exercises : 3 formal metadata encoding/creation exercises will be required. One exercise will focus on descriptive and administrative metadata; another will focus on structural metadata; and the third will focus on crosswalking metadata. There will be a written component for at least one of the segments. The parts of this assignment will each have its own due date.

Metadata schema development : Students will be assembled into small groups and asked to create a metadata schema for a certain class of object. In addition to schema documentation and some form of data dictionary (all executed as part of the group), students will individually write a short paper discussing issues that arose during schema development. The group will present its metadata schema during the last in-person class period.

Course Polices and Procedures:

Formatting of your assignments.

  • Submitted work must be professional presented and/or well written. Check spelling and grammar.
  • For paper assignments, place your name and the assignment number on the first page and in abbreviated form in the header or footer of each page after the first.
  • Minimum margins are 1-inch all around; maximum margins are 1-inch all around.
  • Use double-spaced line spacing, or space and a half, not single-spaced.
  • At this time, work will be submitted electronically. Instructions to follow.

Participation & Conduct:

Attendance. Attendance is mandatory. Arrive on time.

Late work . Each assignment has a stated due date. Assignments turned in after the due time without prior approval will be penalized 10% for each 24-hour period that it is late. Assignments that are over 3 days late will NOT be accepted unless arrangements have been made with me. I encourage you to consult with me any special circumstances that will affect your ability to perform your assignments or turn in your assignments on time.

Makeup work. No makeup work given.

Behave respectfully. Students are expected to behave respectfully and professionally while in class. Participation grades will reflect a student's maturity level and professionalism, and whether the student actively participates in class discussions.

No phone calls during class. Professionals do not field phone calls or texts in meetings.

No grade discussions in class. First consider why the instructors deducted points. If you still disagree, explain your disagreement in an e-mail to the instructors. Schedule a meeting.
