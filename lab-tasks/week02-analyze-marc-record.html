This week's lab task will be to analyze a MARC record.


I've selected 13 MARC records, one for each enrolled student. (If you believe you are enrolled in this course and you do not see your name below, please contact me.) Although the MARC records were selected with some thought, they were assigned randomly. A table below has your name followed by a MARC/XML option and a Text option. Both the XML and Text are the same record and data, I've just provided you two different serializations. This week (and many more) we'll be spending time on XML so start becoming familiar with it.


I would like you to write a 300-400 word analysis of your given MARC record. Although you should focus on your particular MARC record, I strongly encourage you to look at the other MARC records I have chosen. The one you may be writing about might relate to another one, and you'll want to note that.


Something things to consider (this is not an inclusive list):

  • Numeric codes and tags and letter-based subfields. Why might this be? Are there pluses and minuses to this?
  • Does your MARC record represent one thing, more than one thing, a collection of things? How can you tell?
  • What does a MARC represent? Does it represent one single thing? What is it a description of?
  • Does your MARC record or the thing your MARC record describes relate to another MARC record or thing? If so, does the MARC record include some kind of notice about that relationship (structured or in note form)? If it doesn't relate to something and it should, how might that relationship be recorded?
  • Relate your MARC-focused readings to what you see.
  • Are you familiar with FRBR? (If not, read this.) Can you identify a Work, Expression, Manifestation, and Item in your MARC record?
  • Who is a MARC record for? A machine, a human, both? Explain.
I expect a mini-essay, so please do not just extract the questions and provide me with answers.
